Located across the river from Notre Dame Cathedral in Paris, Shakespeare and Company offers a wide range of books and culture to explore.


History and Collections

This famous independent bookstore was founded by American Sylvia Beach in November 1919 in Paris, France. In 1941, the bookstore was closed forever in it's original location after the Nazis returned to the shop to take Ms. Beach to jail after she refused to sell them a book. Another bookstore was re-named Shakespeare and Company in her honor in 1964 on the 400th Anniversary of William Shakespeare's birth. People come from all over the world to experience the atmosphere and take a step back in time. Additional history is available online.


Types of collections you can find in the store include:

  1. Film and Music
  2. Science Fiction
  3. Science and Technology
  4. Art and Fashion
  5. Shakespeare
  6. Travel and so much more.


Madame Marie Curie

A professor at the University of Paris and the founder (along with her husband) of the Curie Institute, Madame Curie won two Nobel Peace prizes for her pioneering research in radiation. Because of this work, she was able to add two new elements to the periodic table.

Some famous Curie quotes include:

  • I was taught that the way of progress was neither swift nor easy.
  • In science, we must be interested in things, not in persons.
  • I am one of those who think like Nobel, that humanity will draw more good than evil from new discoveries.

Ernest Hemmingway

A patron of Shakespeare and Company, Hemingway spent much time in Paris traveling, exploring and gaining inspiration for his next book.

A friendly exchange: "Yes, what can I do for you?" Beach asked. "Do you have any books by Ernest Hemingway?" asked a smiling Ernest. "Yes, we..." Sylvia Beach looked up with an even bigger smile. "Hemingway. Hemingway, my dear, how lovely to see you."

Some of Hemingways top books included:
